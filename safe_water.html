Safe Water Makes A Better Life

A Message from founder Immaculee G. Songa

“In 2012, I traveled to Rwanda from the United States for the commemoration period and went to Nyanza District to pray at a mass grave where some of my family members are buried. I toured my parents’ land and noticed the well that used to provide water for my village was not working anymore; people were getting water from a river. I felt so much pain seeing children get water from that river, but could not do anything at the time. I was lucky enough to travel to Rwanda again, this time bringing my friends from the United States. They saw the need for clean water and decided to help build a well in my village in Nyanza.

I am forever grateful to my friends in the state of Illinois. The well was finished in May 2015 and inaugurated in January 2016. However, when I saw how many children were visiting the well for water, I questioned what I should do next. People told me that the well served three villages and that people walked miles to come get water. I decided then that God has so many ways; the God who provided for that well will provide for other wells to serve other villages, districts, and regions.” – Immaculee G. Songa, founder of Global Survivors for Peace.

Water is essential for life. Unfortunately, access to clean, safe water is not always guaranteed. Especially in places marked by conflict and tragedy, reliable access to life-giving water can be rare, forcing people, oftentimes children, to walk long distances to satisfy this basic need. Their effort is too often futile, as water from unregulated sources may be teeming with diseases.

Risks of Unsafe Water

Lack of safe water sources impacts all areas of life, including health, education, and physical safety. Even if a water source does not hold water-borne diseases, short supply of it means that sanitation such as hand-washing must be forfeited. Children sick from diseases caused by unsafe or insufficient water supplies may not be able to attend school for long periods, or may have to drop out of school altogether, leaving their elementary or secondary education incomplete. Girls who walk long distances for water are at risk of sexual assault and other forms of physical harm. Clean, reliable water prevents many problems for people already traumatized by violence.

Support Clean Water

Global Survivors for Peace’s goal is to raise money and build wells in Rwanda and East Africa. Every person on our planet should have easy access to clean water which prevents diseases, leaves time for school and work, and keeps children and vulnerable people safe. We will partner with local sectors and districts to build wells and bring drinkable water to children, students, the elderly, and those in need. The support from each one of our friends will make a difference in people’s lives and communities. To donate to Global Survivors for Peace’s Safe Water mission, please see Get Involved.

We support survivors in the aftermath of the 1994 Genocide against the Tutsi and other genocides.
Global survivors for peace focuses on three key areas: Supporting survivors, investing in education, and empowering women.
